01 — Brand Story

Built from lived experience

Strollable exists because a parent arrived somewhere only to find the stroller couldn't get through the door. It's that specific friction — invisible until you're standing in front of it — that the product is designed to eliminate.

The brand lives in that moment of relief: knowing before you leave. It's the confidence of a community that's been there, checked, and left a trail for the next parent.

"Not loud. Not precious. Just quietly the most useful thing in your pocket."

07 — Motion & Interaction

Movement that reassures

01
Purposeful
Every animation earns its place. Motion confirms actions, reveals state, or guides attention. Nothing decorative without function.
duration: 200–300ms
02
Organic ease
Avoid linear or bouncy motion. Prefer ease-out for entrances, ease-in for exits. Feels natural, not mechanical.
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
03
Restrained delight
Save expressive animation for milestone moments: badge earned, first contribution, first spot saved. Everyday UI is calm and quiet.
scale 1→1.05 on badge earn
08 — Voice & Tone

Speak like a trusted friend

Strollable is not chirpy. It's not corporate. It speaks plainly, with warmth — like the parent who's already been there and left a note for you.

✓
We sound like this
Direct and specific about what we know
Warm without being saccharine
Honest about uncertainty ("not yet verified")
Community-crediting ("reported by 3 parents")
Quietly confident, never boastful
✕
We don't sound like this
Exclamation points everywhere!
Baby-talk or infantilising language
Vague reassurances ("looks great!")
Corporate passive voice
Over-explained feature descriptions
09 — Brand Do's & Don'ts

Keep it consistent

Do
Use Fraunces for all display type and headings
Lead with Stroller Sage as the dominant colour
Maintain generous white space — the UI breathes
Use the logo on light, dark, or sage backgrounds only
Let data be the hero — design supports, never competes
Use Terracotta sparingly, only for alerts or unverified states
Don't
Stretch, rotate, or recolour the logo mark
Use purple, neon, or high-saturation accent colours
Use system fonts (Arial, Helvetica, San Francisco)
Overlay the logo on busy photography
Mix more than 3 colours in a single screen
Use more than one display typeface at the same time
